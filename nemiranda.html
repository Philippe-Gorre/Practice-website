Nemiranda Arthouse: Where Myths and Legends Breathe

Exploring the "Imaginative Figurism" of Angono’s Master Storyteller.

Nemiranda Arthouse is the creative headquarters of Nemesio "Nemi" Miranda, a legendary figure in the Philippine art world. The structure itself is a work of art, built using local natural materials like bamboo, river stones, and aged wood to reflect a deep connection to the environment.

Quick Info:
Location:

Doña Elena St., Itaas, Angono, Rizal.

Best for: Folklore Fans, Sculptors, and Local Mythologists.
Vibe: Rustic, Mythical, and Bold.


Inside, the galleries are filled with sculptures and paintings that draw inspiration from Filipino folklore, myths, and local legends—depicting everything from the strong "Malakas and Maganda" to the hardworking farmers of the Rizal lowlands. Nemi Miranda’s style is characterized by fluid lines and a sense of movement that brings mythical creatures to life. The arthouse also functions as a community hub, hosting workshops that inspire the next generation of artists. It is a place where the boundaries between reality and legend disappear, offering visitors a chance to see the world through the eyes of a master storyteller who celebrates the Filipino spirit.
