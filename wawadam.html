Wawa Dam: Where History and Myth Converge

Exploring the Historic Gravity Dam and the Legend of Bernardo Carpio.

Wawa Dam is a place of deep historical and cultural significance, nestled between the towering limestone walls of the Rodriguez mountains. Built in 1909, it serves as a beautiful reminder of the region’s past and a gateway to natural adventure.

Quick Info:
Location:

Brgy. San Rafael, Rodriguez (Montalban), Rizal.

Best for: History Enthusiasts, Bamboo Rafting, and Day-Trippers.
Vibe: Rustic, Historical, and Mythical.


Flanked by the massive cliffs of Mt. Pamitinan and Mt. Binacayan, the dam is steeped in the legend of the giant Bernardo Carpio, who is said to have pushed the mountains apart. Today, the dam is a popular spot for sightseeing and bamboo rafting. You can rent a raft and float along the calm river, enjoying the shadow of the white cliffs. The area is also a historical site, having served as a hideout for Filipino revolutionaries during the Japanese occupation. Its mix of American-era engineering and raw limestone beauty makes it a unique stop for those wanting to combine history with a relaxing day by the water.
