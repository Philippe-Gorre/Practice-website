Angono-Binangonan Petroglyphs

Angono-Binangonan Petroglyphs: Echoes from 3000 BC

Witness the Oldest Known Artworks in the Philippines.

Stepping into this site is like traveling back five thousand years to the dawn of Philippine civilization. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, these carvings are the oldest known artworks in the archipelago, consisting of 127 stylized figures etched into a volcanic rock wall.

Quick Info:
Location:

Binangonan-Angono Boundary, Rizal.

Best for: History Buffs, Students, and Cultural Explorers.
Vibe: Ancient, Mysterious, and Educational.


These prehistoric engravings offer an invaluable glimpse into the belief systems and daily lives of late Neolithic inhabitants long before the arrival of Spanish colonizers. Visitors reach the rock shelter through a man-made tunnel that opens into a protected viewing deck. The ancient figures—some dancing, some hunting—stand as silent witnesses to the passage of time. The site is managed by the National Museum of the Philippines and serves as a profound reminder that the spirit of Filipino artistry has been thriving in the hills of Rizal since the beginning of history.
